# Tebra vs AdvancedMD: which is right for your independent practice?

<!-- block:quick-answer id="quick-answer-1" -->
## The short answer

For solo-to-10-provider independent practices, Tebra and AdvancedMD target the same buyer. The practical split is platform architecture. Tebra bundles EHR, practice management, billing, and patient engagement into one unified system with AI-assisted charting, while AdvancedMD ships a more modular platform whose strongest, most-publicly-priced offering is outsourced revenue cycle management at a percentage of collections. Pick Tebra if your priority is a single-system workflow and one vendor relationship; pick AdvancedMD if your priority is modular flexibility or outsourced RCM.

## How the platforms actually differ

If you run a solo-to-10-provider practice, the daily question is not which vendor has more features on a spec sheet. It is how many systems your staff has to log into, reconcile, and troubleshoot before lunch. That is the axis where Tebra and AdvancedMD split.

### Tebra: one unified platform, one vendor

Tebra sells a bundled platform. You buy the EHR, practice management, billing and payments, patient experience, and practice marketing as one product, from one vendor, on one login.

<!-- block:proof id="proof-platform-architecture" cite="src_tebra_features_public_2026" -->
Tebra bundles EHR, billing and payments, patient experience, and practice marketing into a single unified platform built for private practices, requiring no additional systems.

<!-- /block -->

That architecture decision has second-order effects. When your billing team opens a claim, the clinical note, the charge, the patient's balance, and the eligibility check all sit in the same record. Nobody rekeys data between systems. Nobody debugs a broken integration at month-end close.

### AdvancedMD: modular components plus a partner ecosystem

AdvancedMD takes a different approach. The product is sold as a set of components you can assemble, and the company operates a partner program so you can pair the software with a third-party billing company rather than a single bundled vendor.

<!-- block:proof id="proof-advancedmd-modular" cite="src_advancedmd_home_2026" -->
AdvancedMD positions itself as a modular medical office software platform (offering practice management, EHR, and patient engagement as components) and operates an AdvancedBiller Grow partner program for third-party billing companies, enabling practices to work with external RCM partners rather than a single bundled vendor.

<!-- /block -->

That modularity is a feature, not a bug, for practices that already have a billing vendor relationship they trust, or that want the freedom to swap components later. The tradeoff is that every added component is another contract, another login, and another integration to maintain.

## Where Tebra's bundled approach shows up in the workflow

### AI-assisted charting that reduces note-taking time

Documentation is the single largest time sink for independent practice clinicians. Tebra's EHR attacks this directly with AI plus reusable content.

<!-- block:proof id="proof-ai-charting" cite="src_tebra_ehr_public_2026" -->
Tebra EHR includes AI-powered charting with AI Note Assist, reusable templates, macros, and SALT (pull-forward past visit content) to reduce documentation time.

<!-- /block -->

The practical effect for a solo provider is that your chart closes at the end of the visit, not at 9 p.m. from the kitchen table. For a five-to-ten-provider practice, that gap compounds into measurable capacity.

### Billing automation that runs inside the same system as the chart

Because Tebra's billing lives on the same platform as the EHR, the automation fires on the same record clinicians are already touching.

<!-- block:proof id="proof-billing-rpa" cite="src_tebra_billing_public_2026" -->
Tebra billing includes robotic process automation (RPA) for ERA posting, A/R updates, and data transfers, plus electronic claim submission and automated patient statements, all within the same platform as the EHR.

<!-- /block -->

This matters most at two points in your month: ERA posting day (the RPA handles the match-and-post work your biller used to do by hand) and statement run (patients receive statements automatically, without a separate batch job in a separate system).

### Patient-facing tools in the same login

Patient engagement is where most EHRs show their age: bolted-on portals, separate scheduling tools, text-reminder vendors that do not talk to the chart. Tebra puts these in the same login.

<!-- block:proof id="proof-patient-experience" cite="src_tebra_patient_experience_public_2026" -->
Tebra patient experience tools (including online scheduling, automated reminders, two-way text communication, digital patient intake, and AI review replies) are natively integrated with the EHR and billing layers in one login.

<!-- /block -->

When a patient books online, the slot reserves against the provider's live schedule; when they complete intake on their phone, the answers land in the chart, not in a PDF your front desk has to rekey.

## Where AdvancedMD has an edge: transparent outsourced RCM

Honest tradeoff: AdvancedMD's outsourced billing service is one of the more transparently described offerings in the market, and for practices that want to hand revenue cycle to an external team, it is worth a serious look.

<!-- block:proof id="proof-advancedmd-rcm" cite="src_advancedmd_rcm_pricing_2026" -->
AdvancedMD outsourced RCM services are priced as a percent of monthly collections (typically ~4–8%), with the billing team managing claims submissions, payer follow-up, denial appeals, and secondary filings on the practice's behalf.

<!-- /block -->

If your practice does not want to staff an in-house biller and you prefer a variable cost tied to what you actually collect, that model is legitimately hard to beat. Similarly, if you already have a billing company you like, the AdvancedBiller Grow partner program lets you keep that relationship while adopting the software. Neither of those is a fit Tebra's bundled model is designed for.

## Frequently asked questions

### Does Tebra integrate EHR and billing in one system?

Yes. Tebra's EHR, practice management, and billing and payments modules run on a single platform with one login. Claims, charges, clinical notes, and patient balances live on the same record, so your team does not rekey data or reconcile across separate systems.

### How does AdvancedMD's pricing work for a solo practice?

AdvancedMD software pricing is available by quote request, not published on the site. The more publicly described pricing is for the outsourced RCM service, which is billed as a percent of monthly collections (typically in the ~4–8% range). For a solo practice, that means your software and billing-service costs can vary based on which components you buy and whether you outsource revenue cycle.

### Is Tebra or AdvancedMD better for a solo practice?

It depends on what you are optimizing for. If you want one vendor, one login, and a bundled platform that covers EHR, billing, and patient engagement, Tebra is built for that use case. If you want modular flexibility, the ability to keep or bring your own billing company, or a percent-of-collections outsourced RCM model, AdvancedMD is the stronger fit.

### Can I get published pricing for either vendor online?

Tebra publishes plan tiers on its pricing page. AdvancedMD uses a quote-request model for its software and publishes the percent-of-collections range for its outsourced RCM service. If transparent software pricing is a hard requirement before you take a sales call, Tebra's model is closer to that preference.

### Does Tebra replace an existing billing company?

It can, because billing and payments are included in the Tebra platform. If you already work with a billing company and want to keep that relationship, AdvancedMD's AdvancedBiller Grow partner program is designed for that scenario; Tebra's model assumes you want billing bundled with the software.
