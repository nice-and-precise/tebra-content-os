# Tebra Brand Voice

## Overview

Tebra's voice is confident, precise, and human — the trusted colleague who helps independent practices run better, not a corporate platform talking down at clinicians.

## Tone Principles

**Confident without arrogance.** State what Tebra does and why it matters. Don't hedge with "we think" or oversell with "revolutionary."

**Precise over general.** "Reduces claim denial rate by 23%" beats "improves revenue cycle." If you don't have a number, name the mechanism.

**Human over clinical.** Clinicians are smart humans running businesses. Write for the business problem, not the EHR feature set.

**Practitioner-first framing.** Lead with the practice's outcome, then the Tebra capability that produces it. Never lead with a product name or feature.

## Cadence Rules

- **Sentence length:** 12–18 words average. One idea per sentence. Break any sentence over 25 words.
- **Paragraph length:** 2–4 sentences maximum. One concept per paragraph.
- **Active voice:** Subject → verb → object. Passive constructions ("is being processed by") are banned.
- **Second person:** Address the reader as "you" or "your practice." Never "the customer" or "users."
- **Numbers:** Spell out one through nine; numerals for 10 and above. Percentages always use numerals ("7%", not "seven percent").

## Banned Words and Phrases

| Banned | Use instead |
|--------|-------------|
| Revolutionary / game-changing | Measurable / proven |
| Seamless | Automatic / one-step / (describe the actual flow) |
| Leverage | Use |
| Utilize | Use |
| Solution | Product / platform / tool (be specific) |
| World-class | (delete — show the evidence instead) |
| Cutting-edge | Current / latest / (name the feature) |
| Empower | Help / enable / give you |
| Synergy | (delete always) |
| Robust | (delete — be specific about what it handles) |
| Healthcare journey | Patient care / care delivery |

## Healthcare-Specific Language Constraints

- **No disease management claims** without a source citation. "Reduces A1C" requires an approved source registered in `/sources/registry.json`.
- **No patient outcome projections** (e.g., "patients will see better outcomes"). Rephrase as practice-efficiency outcomes.
- **Competitor names:** Use exact legal name on first mention; abbreviate on second. Never use a competitor's brand as a verb.
- **Regulatory terms (HIPAA, FDA, CMS):** Use accurately. Do not imply certification or endorsement unless it exists in a registered source.
- **"AI" attribution:** Describe what the AI does, not how smart it is. "Automatically flags claim codes" not "AI-powered intelligence."

## Structural Defaults

- **Answer first.** State the conclusion in the first sentence of every section.
- **Proof next.** Follow each assertion with a source citation or measurable outcome.
- **CTA last.** Every content piece ends with one clear action step — no stacked CTAs.

## Common Mistakes

| Mistake | Fix |
|---------|-----|
| Leading with Tebra feature name | Start with the practice pain or outcome |
| Stacking superlatives | Pick one differentiator and prove it |
| Generic healthcare stats | Stats must be sourced and registered in `/sources/` |
| Passive constructions | Rewrite: "Tebra processes claims" not "claims are processed" |
| Vague timeframe ("quickly", "soon") | Specify: "in under 24 hours", "same-day" |
